## 📝 Documents Typically Required

### For Competitive Scholarships (Chevening, Vanier, Gates)
- [ ] Academic transcripts (all degrees, certified)
- [ ] Degree certificates
- [ ] CV/Resume — 2 pages, academic format
- [ ] Personal statement / Scholarship essay — 500-1,000 words
- [ ] Leadership statement
- [ ] Research proposal (for research scholarships)
- [ ] 2-3 Letters of Recommendation (scholarship-specific referees)
- [ ] Proof of English proficiency
- [ ] Passport copy
- [ ] Employment / work experience proof (Chevening needs 2+ yrs)
- [ ] Community/leadership evidence
- [ ] Publications (if applicable)

### For Auto-Assessed University Scholarships
- Automatically considered based on your program application
- Submit strong academic CV and transcripts
- Some require a one-page statement of merit

## 💡 10 Tips for Winning Scholarships

1. **Start early** — Competitive scholarships (Chevening, Vanier, Gates) require 2-4 months of prep
2. **Tell a compelling story** — Don't just list achievements; show your journey and transformation
3. **Demonstrate impact** — How will this education allow you to help your community or country?
4. **Leadership is non-negotiable** — Every major scholarship values demonstrated leadership
5. **Get targeted LORs** — Choose referees who can speak to the specific qualities each scholarship values
6. **Match your goals to the scholarship mission** — Read the funder's values and mirror them authentically
7. **Apply broadly** — Target 3 P1 scholarships, 5 P2, and all auto-assessed ones
8. **Follow word counts and formats exactly** — Going over = instant disqualification for many
9. **Have someone else review** — Typos and unclear prose undermine otherwise strong applications
10. **Be authentic** — Scholarship committees read thousands of essays; genuine voices stand out
