## 📝 Application Documents Checklist

### Required for All
- [ ] Academic transcripts (all degrees)
- [ ] Degree certificates
- [ ] CV/Resume
- [ ] Statement of Purpose
- [ ] 2-3 Letters of Recommendation
- [ ] IELTS/TOEFL score
- [ ] Research proposal (thesis-based programs)
- [ ] Writing sample (some programs)
- [ ] Passport copy

### Canada-Specific
- [ ] Study permit application (after offer)
- [ ] Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL)
- [ ] Biometrics
- [ ] Medical exam
- [ ] GIC (Guaranteed Investment Certificate) - CAD $20,635
- [ ] Proof of funds for first year

### Important Notes
- **GRE:** Generally NOT required for Canadian CS programs (unlike US)
- **Funding:** Many MSc programs offer TA/RA positions that cover tuition + stipend
- **Co-op:** Waterloo and UBC have the strongest co-op programs
- **Bilingual:** McGill is in Montreal (French-speaking province) but CS is English-taught

### TA/RA Funding
Most Canadian MSc programs offer:
- **TA (Teaching Assistant):** CAD $15,000-25,000/yr + tuition waiver
- **RA (Research Assistant):** CAD $20,000-30,000/yr + tuition waiver
- **Combined:** Often covers full tuition + living stipend

### Immigration Pathway
1. **Study Permit** → Study (2 years)
2. **PGWP (Post-Graduation Work Permit)** → Work (3 years)
3. **Express Entry (CEC)** → Apply for PR
4. **Permanent Residency** → Citizenship (after 3 years)

**Key Advantage:** Canadian work experience through PGWP counts toward Express Entry. Most graduates get PR within 1-2 years of starting work.
