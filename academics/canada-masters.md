## 📋 Application Documents Checklist

### Required for All
- [ ] Academic transcripts (all degrees, official sealed copies)
- [ ] Degree certificates
- [ ] CV/Resume (2 pages)
- [ ] Statement of Purpose (research-based: 2-3 pages; course-based: 1-2 pages)
- [ ] Research proposal (thesis-based programs — 2-3 pages)
- [ ] 2-3 Letters of Recommendation (academic referees preferred)
- [ ] IELTS Academic / TOEFL score
- [ ] Writing sample (some programs — check individually)
- [ ] Passport copy

### Canada-Specific (After Offer)
- [ ] Study Permit application — CAD $150 + biometrics CAD $85
- [ ] Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL) — from province via university
- [ ] Biometrics appointment
- [ ] Medical exam (if from listed country or staying 6+ months)
- [ ] Police clearance certificate
- [ ] GIC — CAD $20,635 (Guaranteed Investment Certificate)
- [ ] Proof of funds: Tuition + CAD $20,635/yr living expenses

### Important Notes
- **GRE:** Generally NOT required for Canadian CS programs
- **Funding:** Most MSc thesis programs offer TA/RA that covers tuition + CAD $15,000–25,000/yr stipend
- **PAL:** New requirement as of Jan 2024 — university requests on your behalf
- **Co-op:** Requires separate open work permit; co-op counts toward PGWP work experience

### Immigration Pathway
1. **Study Permit** → Study (2 years MSc)
2. **PGWP** → Work for 3 years, any employer (matches program length up to 3 yrs)
3. **Express Entry (CEC)** → 1 year Canadian skilled work experience → PR draw
4. **Permanent Residency** → Apply ~2 years into work
5. **Citizenship** → 3 years after PR

**Key Advantage:** PGWP = open work permit. Canadian work experience = points in Express Entry. Most MSc graduates reach PR within 3-4 years of arrival.
