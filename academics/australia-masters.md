## 📅 Intake Dates
Australia has **2 main intakes** per year + some with a 3rd intake:
- **Term 1:** Starts February → Apply by **November 30** previous year
- **Term 2:** Starts July → Apply by **May 31** same year
- **Term 3 (some unis):** Starts November → Apply by **August 31**

## 📋 Application Documents Checklist

### Required for All
- [ ] Academic transcripts (all degrees, certified)
- [ ] Degree certificates
- [ ] CV/Resume (2 pages)
- [ ] Statement of Purpose (1-2 pages)
- [ ] 2 Letters of Recommendation (some only need 1)
- [ ] IELTS Academic / PTE Academic / TOEFL score
- [ ] Passport copy

### Australia-Specific (After Offer)
- [ ] Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) — issued by university after acceptance
- [ ] **Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) statement** — must demonstrate genuine study intent
- [ ] **Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)** — purchase before applying for visa
- [ ] Student Visa Subclass 500 application — AUD $1,620
- [ ] Financial evidence: Tuition + AUD $24,505/yr living + AUD $2,000-3,000 travel
- [ ] Health examination (if required by immigration)
- [ ] Police clearance certificate

### Important Notes
- **GTE is critical:** Many applications rejected for weak GTE statements
- **OSHC mandatory:** Must purchase before lodging Student Visa application
- **Work rights:** 48 hrs/fortnight during term; unlimited during semester breaks
- **No GRE** required at any Australian university
- **Multiple intakes:** More flexibility than UK/Canada

### Immigration Pathway (Updated 2025)
1. **Student Visa (500)** → Study 2 years
2. **Temporary Graduate Visa (485) — Post-Study Work Stream:** 
   - 2 years (bachelor's / coursework masters)
   - 3 years (research masters)
   - 4 years (PhD)
   - **Note:** Students who studied in regional Australia get +2 extra years
3. **Employer Nomination Scheme (ENS) or Skilled Independent (189)** → Permanent Residency
4. **General Skilled Migration (GSM)** → PR via points test (need 65+ points)
5. **Citizenship** → After 4 years (1 as PR)

**Key Advantage:** Points-based system favors young, skilled, English-proficient graduates. Masters adds 20 points. Australian study adds 5 extra points.
