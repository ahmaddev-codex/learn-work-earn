## 💰 Funding & Scholarships

| Scholarship | Amount | Duration | Eligibility | Deadline |
|-------------|--------|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Vanier CGS** | CAD $50,000/yr | 3 years | PhD students | Nov 2026 |
| **NSERC CGS-D** | CAD $35,000/yr | 3 years | PhD students | Oct 2026 |
| **Ontario Trillium Scholarship** | CAD $40,000/yr | 4 years | Ontario PhD students | Varies |
| **University Fellowships** | Full funding | 4-5 years | All admitted PhD students | Auto |
| **TA/RA Positions** | CAD $25,000-35,000/yr | Ongoing | All PhD students | Auto |

### Key Funding Notes
- **ALL Canadian PhD programs guarantee full funding** for admitted students
- **Funding covers:** Tuition + living stipend + health insurance
- **No separate funding application** needed at most universities (auto-considered)
- **External scholarships** (Vanier, NSERC) provide additional prestige and higher stipends

## 📝 Application Strategy

### Timeline
- **12 months before:** Research labs and supervisors
- **9 months before:** Email supervisors, discuss research fit
- **6 months before:** Draft research proposal
- **4 months before:** Request LORs, prepare transcripts
- **3 months before:** Submit application
- **2 months before:** Apply for external scholarships

### Documents Required
- [ ] Academic transcripts (all degrees)
- [ ] CV/Resume
- [ ] Statement of Purpose
- [ ] Research Proposal (2,000-3,000 words)
- [ ] 3 Letters of Recommendation
- [ ] IELTS/TOEFL score
- [ ] GRE score (if recommended)
- [ ] Writing sample (some programs)
- [ ] Publications (if any)

## 🏢 Immigration Advantage

PhD graduates in Canada have **significant immigration advantages:**
- **Express Entry:** PhD holders get maximum education points
- **Provincial Nominee Programs:** Many provinces have PhD-specific streams
- **PGWP:** 3-year work permit after PhD
- **PR Timeline:** Most PhD graduates get PR within 1-2 years of graduation
- **Research Experience:** Counts toward Canadian work experience
