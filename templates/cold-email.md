# 📧 Cold Email Template

> Template for reaching out to professors for research opportunities, PhD supervision, or collaboration.

---

## 🎯 General Structure

1. **Subject Line** - Clear and specific
2. **Greeting** - Professional, use correct title
3. **Hook** - Why you're writing to THEM specifically
4. **Background** - Brief intro of yourself
5. **Fit** - Why you're interested in their work
6. **Ask** - Clear, specific request
7. **Attach** - CV/Resume
8. **Close** - Professional sign-off

---

## Template 1: PhD Supervision Inquiry

### Subject Line Options
- PhD Inquiry: [Your Name] - [Research Area] - [Year]
- Prospective PhD Student Interested in [Research Area]
- Application for PhD Supervision - [Your Name]
- Research Interest in [Specific Paper/Topic] - PhD Inquiry

### Email Body

```
Dear Professor [Last Name],

I hope this email finds you well. My name is [Your Name], and I am writing to 
inquire about PhD supervision opportunities in your research group at 
[University Name].

I recently read your paper "[Paper Title]" published in [Venue, Year], and I 
was particularly fascinated by [specific finding/technique/contribution]. 
[Add 2-3 sentences showing you understood the paper and its implications].

I am currently [your current status - e.g., a final-year BSc student at 
[University] / a Master's student at [University] / working as a [Role] at 
[Company]]. My research interests align closely with your work in 
[Research Area], particularly [specific sub-topic]. 

My relevant background includes:
- [Relevant coursework/project 1]
- [Relevant coursework/project 2]
- [Any publications, if applicable]
- [Relevant skills: programming languages, tools, frameworks]

I am particularly interested in exploring [specific research question or 
direction] and believe your expertise in [area] would be invaluable for 
this work. I have attached my CV for your reference.

Would you be open to a brief call or email exchange to discuss potential 
PhD opportunities in your group? I would be happy to work around your schedule.

Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Email]
[Your Phone Number]
[LinkedIn Profile URL]
[GitHub Profile URL (if relevant)]
```

## Template 2: Masters Program Inquiry

- Masters Inquiry: [Program Name] - [Your Name]

- Application for MSc Supervision - [Your Name]

### Email Body

```
Dear Professor [Last Name],

I hope you are doing well. My name is [Your Name], and I am writing to 
express my interest in the [MSc Program Name] at [University Name], 
specifically under your supervision.

I came across your work on [Research Area] through [Paper/Project/Website], 
and I was impressed by [specific aspect of their work]. Your approach to 
[specific technique/method] resonates strongly with my academic interests.

I am currently [your current status] with a strong foundation in 
[relevant subjects/skills]. My academic record includes [CGPA/GPA], and I 
have experience with [relevant projects/research/work].

I am particularly drawn to [University Name] because of [specific reason - 
reputation, research facilities, specific course, location]. I believe 
that working with you would provide the ideal environment for me to 
develop my skills in [Research Area].

I have attached my CV and transcript for your review. I would be grateful 
for the opportunity to discuss my application with you further.

Thank you for considering my inquiry. I look forward to your response.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Email]
[Your Phone Number]
[LinkedIn Profile URL]
```

## Template 3: Research Collaboration (for current students/researchers)

### Subject Line Options
- Collaboration Inquiry: [Research Area] - [Your Name]
- Interested in Collaborating on [Topic] - [Your Name]
- Research Collaboration Opportunity - [Your Name]

### Email Body

```
Dear Professor [Last Name],

I hope this message finds you well. My name is [Your Name], and I am 
currently [your position] at [Institution].

I have been following your research on [Research Area] with great interest, 
particularly your recent work on [specific paper/project]. I believe there 
is an exciting opportunity for collaboration between our research groups 
on [specific topic/direction].

My current research focuses on [Your Research Area], and I have recently 
[published/worked on] [brief description of your work]. I see potential 
for synergy with your work on [their work], particularly in [specific 
overlap area].

I would be delighted to discuss potential collaboration opportunities, 
whether through a joint paper, research visit, or student exchange. 
Would you be available for a brief video call to explore this further?

I have attached my recent publications and CV for your reference.

Thank you for your time, and I look forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Title/Position]
[Institution]
[Email]
[Website/Google Scholar Profile]
```

## Template 4: Follow-Up Email (after no response)

### Subject Line Options
- Follow-Up: [Original Subject Line]
- Re: [Original Subject Line]
- Checking In: PhD/MSc Inquiry - [Your Name]

### Email Body

```
Dear Professor [Last Name],

I hope you are doing well. I am writing to follow up on my email from 
[Date] regarding [PhD/MSc/Research] opportunities in your group.

I understand you receive many inquiries, and I wanted to ensure my message 
didn't get lost in your inbox. I remain very interested in [specific 
research area] and would greatly appreciate any guidance you could offer.

If you are not currently accepting students, I would be grateful for any 
recommendations you might have for other researchers in the field.

Thank you again for your time and consideration.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Email]
```

## ✅ Email Checklist Before Sending

- [ ] Subject line is clear and specific
- [ ] Professor's name is spelled correctly
- [ ] University name is correct
- [ ] Paper/project reference is accurate
- [ ] No spelling or grammar errors
- [ ] Email is concise (under 300 words ideally)
- [ ] CV/Resume is attached (PDF format)
- [ ] Links work (LinkedIn, GitHub, etc.)
- [ ] Contact information is correct
- [ ] Sent from professional email address
- [ ] Checked professor's website for specific instructions

---

## ❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Mass emailing** - Never send the same email to multiple professors
2. **Generic emails** - Always customize for each professor
3. **Too long** - Keep it under 300 words
4. **No research** - Don't contact professors without reading their work
5. **Vague asks** - Be specific about what you want
6. **Missing attachments** - Always attach CV
7. **Wrong title** - Verify if they are Prof/Dr/Assoc Prof
8. **Typos** - Proofread multiple times
9. **Follow up too soon** - Wait 2 weeks before following up
10. **Follow up too many times** - Max 2 follow-ups

## 📊 Response Rate Expectations

| Professor Tier | Expected Response Rate | Timeline |
|---------------|----------------------|----------|
| **Top-tier (very famous)** | 10-20% | 2-8 weeks |
| **Well-known** | 20-40% | 1-4 weeks |
| **Mid-career** | 40-60% | 1-2 weeks |
| **Early career** | 60-80% | 1-2 weeks |

**Don't be discouraged by non-responses!** Professors are extremely busy. 
A non-response is not a rejection.
